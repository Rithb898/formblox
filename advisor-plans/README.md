## Findings considered and rejected (round 2 — do not re-audit)

- **"Unverified re-signup enables account takeover"** (subagent claim) —
  FALSE. `packages/services/auth/index.ts:42` throws `EMAIL_TAKEN` for any
  verified email; overwriting an *unverified* account is the standard
  anti-squatting pattern and the verification email still goes to the real
  mailbox owner. By design.
- **Missing composite index on form_versions(form_id, status)** — the partial
  unique indexes `one_draft_per_form` / `one_published_per_form`
  (packages/database/models/form-versions.ts:43-48) already cover exactly the
  hot lookups. Not worth doing.
- **Unlisted forms accessible via direct slug** — that is the definition of
  "unlisted" in hackathon.md ("Only accessible via direct link"). By design.
- **setVisibility missing formSlug cache invalidation** — real omission but
  near-zero impact alone (getBySlug neither returns nor gates on visibility;
  submit reads the DB fresh). Folded into plan 005 as a one-line consistency
  fix rather than its own plan.
- **Fire-and-forget owner-notification email (public.ts:204-240)** — adding a
  queue/retry is over-engineering at this product stage; best-effort +
  console.error is an acceptable, common pattern here. Not worth doing now;
  revisit if notifications become a paid feature.
- **`config` cast on read (versions.ts:34)** — LOW-confidence smell; configs
  are schema-validated on write. Not worth doing.
- **listPublic COUNT(DISTINCT) cost** — mitigated by 300s cache; profile
  before optimizing. Deferred, no plan.
- **Pre-commit hooks** — operator taste. Skipped.
- **rate-limit duplication (apps/web/lib/rate-limit.ts vs services/redis)** —
  intentional, documented in the round-1 plans (web copy fails open by
  design). Settled.
